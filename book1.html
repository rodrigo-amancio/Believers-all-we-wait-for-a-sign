In the beginning, from the formless void and the darkness of the deep, from the intensive forces that in three minutes created all matter, were established the principles that would ultimately regulate evolution.
Let there be light, since energy generated matter, matter generated life, life generated intelligence—intelligence generated Posthumanity.

But matter is not substance. In the Cartesian tradition, substance corresponds to the non-extended God separated from the physical world of nature. The split between the mind and the body originated from the separation of the cosmos from matter, of the transcendent God—the power of the soul–mind—from nature—the power of the physical body. The body originates in God as God corresponds to an intensive and extensive substance. Matter is able to manifest itself through the ceaseless mutation of bodies and things in nature.

The laws of the universe have these attributes because that is their nature. They are absolute and they are eternal, as they are omnipotent and omniscient; they affect physical states, but are not affected by them; they do not change with time and they automatically exert their force anywhere in the universe, not needing the systems to inform them of their own existence.
By exploring the prospect of a universal belief, we deny organized religions that perpetuate structures of power and dominance, as we deny the creation of conflict. We contest the creationist emergence of humanity, the conception of an anthropomorphic God. Our evolutionary purpose is linked to human genesis as well as its future. Where do we come from? Where are we going? If such a doctrine were to be spread, what will become of the unchangeable dogmas, what of the stability of the Creed? We seek a transcendent harmony, an intelligent presence, a universal force. Life evolved before humans even existed, evolution does not end with mankind.
